College of Charleston Mock Trial Constitution

We, the members of the College of Charleston Mock Trial, to fully realize our purpose, do hereby adopt and establish this constitution as the guiding instrument of our Club.

ARTICLE I: NAME & PURPOSE

Section A: Name – The name of this club shall be College of Charleston Mock Trial.
Section B: National Affiliation – The rules and regulations of the American Mock Trial Association shall be followed when not inconsistent with the rules and regulations of College of Charleston.
Section C: Purpose – The purpose of this club shall be:

  1. To allow undergraduate students to engage in trial simulations and compete with other colleges and universities across the nation at the regional, state, and national levels.
  2. To help students develop critical thinking and public speaking skills.
  3. To encourage a knowledge of legal practices and procedures.

ARTICLE II: MEMBERSHIP

Section A: Eligibility - Membership shall be open to all College of Charleston students.

ARTICLE III: OFFICERS

Section A: Officers – The officers shall be a President, Vice-President, Secretary, Communications Director, and Treasurer.
Section B: Eligibility – Officers must be undergraduate students at the College of Charleston enrolled in at least twelve credit hours.
Section C: Election – The officers shall be elected by ballot at the end of the spring semester by a majority of the vote cast for that office. The current officers will select the date for the election and an officer not returning the following year will create the ballot and supervise the election.
Section D: Term – The officers shall serve for one year and their term of office shall begin after the current officers and newly elected officers meet to exchange information and transfer duties.
Section E: Vacancy – If a vacancy occurs in the office of President, the Vice-President shall assume the office for the remainder of the term and vacancies in any other office shall be filled by special election, filled by appointing a replacement, or left vacant until the next election per the decision of the remaining officers.
Section F: Removal – If an officer fails to complete the duties listed below, the President and Vice President can decide to remove them from their duties and follow the procedures listed in Section E. The officer may appeal this decision, in which case, they must meet with the entire Executive Committee, who will vote by anonymous ballot on whether to keep or remove the officer. In the event of a tie, the officer will be given a three-week period to prove themselves able to complete the duties of their position. If they do not complete any of the required duties, they will be removed from office. Removed officers must wait two years before running for another executive officer position.

ARTICLE IV: DUTIES OF OFFICERS


Section A: President – it shall be the duty of the President to:
  • Leads executive board and the club overall by example
  • Ultimately, is the one to make the decisions
  • Acts as a liaison among community leaders, other Mock Trial clubs around the nation and SC, and other CofC clubs
  • Manages fundraising and donations with the Treasurer
  • Co-writes endorsements, press releases, and other public statements with VP
  • Table/attend every event (2 absences allowed per semester)
Section B: Vice-President – It shall be the duty of the Vice-President to:
  • Oversees/plans events and their management
  • Leads meetings and events
  • Table/attend every event (2 absences allowed per semester)
  • Co-writes endorsements, press releases, and other public statements with President
  • Recruits new members
Section C: Secretary – It shall be the duty of the Secretary to:
  • Communicates with club members about events, opportunities, and updates of the club
  • Attends Student Government Association events that pertain to Secretary and maintains Cougar Connect page
  • Creates, organizes, and maintains all sign-in sheets → keeps track of members and their contact information
  • Takes attendance
  • Table/attend every event (3 absences allowed per semester)
Section D: Treasurer – It shall be the duty of the Treasurer to:
  • Attends budget workshops for Student Government Association on behalf of CofC Mock Trial
  • Drafts and maintains budget for each semester
  • Submits budgets to SGA for approval each semester
  • Communicates with LaVerne regarding finances and purchases
  • Manages fundraising and donations with the President
  • Table/attend every event (3 absences allowed per semester)
Section D: Public Relations/Fundraising Head – It shall be the duty of the Public Relations/Fundraising Head to:
  • Manage all social media accounts (Instagram and Twitter)
  • Creates flyers/posters/designs for marketing events, meetings, opportunities, etc.
  • Reaches out to members if the secretary is ever unable to
  • Table/attend every event (3 absences allowed per semester)
Section E: Technology Coordinator – It shall be the duty of the Technology Coordinator to:
  • Oversees/manages club website
  • Create QR codes for flyers and posters
  • Logs evidence in each case and keeps digital & physical records of exhibits
  • Fields questions and suggestions for website content and code upkeep

ARTICLE V: MEETINGS

Section A: Meetings – The officers shall determine how frequent meetings should be held, but it should not be less than once a month.
Section B: Special Meeting – Special meetings may be called by the President with the approval of the Executive Committee.

ARTICLE VI: EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE

Section A: Responsibility – Management of this club shall be vested in an Executive Committee responsible to the entire membership to uphold these bylaws.
Section B: Membership – This committee shall consist of the officers as listed in Article III and the faculty advisor.
Section C: Meetings – This committee shall meet at least once between regular meetings of the club to organize and plan future activities.

ARTICLE VII: AMENDMENTS

Section A: Selection – These bylaws may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the Executive Committee.
Section B: Notice – All members shall receive advance notice of the proposed amendment at least five days before the meeting.
